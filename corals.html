Coral Bleaching

tl;dr

My project partner and I developed a series of data visualizations in the form of a journalistic article (a la pudding.cool and the New York Times) about global coral health trends. Along the way, we explored a variety of datasets and visualization tools including Tableau, Vega-Lite, and D3.

March 2020

Role: Developer

Tools: D3, Tableau, JavaScript, HTML/CSS

Read our article here!

Problem

We were interested in trends related to climate change and environmental indicators. We found several databases containing information on coral reefs, coral bleaching, and coral diseases. Since we knew that coral reefs are important to many complicated ocean ecosystems and are sensitive to climate change, we wanted to investigate how the health of various coral reefs have changed over time, and how they may be impacted by human factors.

Solution

We began by exploring different datasets to gain a better understanding of the problem space and the possibilities for visual explainers. We looked at datasets that were related to the global coral health, including ones about sea temperatures, diseases, and bleaching events. We settled on our key datasets and performed data manipulation and wrangling, such as removing data for years with few data points and reorganizing data for more efficient processing. We used tools like Trifacta and Python here.

Next, we performed initial data exploration using Tableau, experimenting with using different marks and encodings to highlight trends, representing information in different types of charts, and testing out small multiples and maps.

We developed a total of 3 interactive data visualizations using D3, JavaScript, HTML, and CSS. One explains coral bleaching events over time, another shows different coral families around the globe, and the last one shows trends in different coral diseases over time.
