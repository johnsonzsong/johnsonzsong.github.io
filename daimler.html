Daimler In-Vehicle Gaming

tl;dr

We partnered with Daimler to create an online game playable on a Mercedes Benz Head Unit. We had the unique opportunity to take full control, from ideation to design to development, of creating an in-vehicle gaming experience for the autonomous driving era.

January - June 2020

Role: Lead Product Designer and Front-end Engineer

Tools: React, Figma

Play Cabana Trivia here!

Problem

As autonomy and electric vehicle charging become more established in the automotive industry, people will have more time on their hands. Whether waiting for their cars to charge or waiting to pick up a child from school, drivers will have an opportunity to engage in in-vehicle entertainment during these times.

Process

User Studies

Our partners are Daimler wanted to know: what kind of games would be good in the car?

My team began by conducting research and user studies to discover these insights. We observed Tesla owners at Supercharger stations to get a better understanding of how owners of autonomous and electric vehicles spent those moments of down-time while waiting for their vehicles to charge.

I ventured to a nearby Tesla dealership to learn more about the current status of in-car gaming. I was able to sit in different models and inspect Tesla display units, as well as play around with their games such as Stardew Valley. Before I left, I got the chance to test-drive the Tesla Model 3 around the neighborhood!

Ideation

My team then went through many ideation stages, coming up with as many games as possible and then narrowing them down based on our findings and constraints. Some of our initial ideas from the first brainstorming session included board games like LIFE and Monopoly, immersive experiences like Mario Kart and Pokemon Go, and old school games like Pac-Man and Space Invaders.

In the second round of brainstorming, we focused on aspects of the in-car gaming experience we wanted to emphasis. We knew that the game should capitalize on the unique circumstances and people inside of the car. Some of our ideas here included trivia, karaoke, 20 questions, and Mafia. With helpful feedback from our Daimler liaisons, we decided to move forward with 3 ideas: trivia, karaoke, and Draw Something.

Prototypes and Findings

We rapidly-prototyped these 3 ideas by creating quick pen-and-paper versions of each game, which meant to test the interactions that friends and family playing together would have. We set up the environment to mimic the inside of a car, and recruited participants that represented the demographic we were aiming for. Our key insights were:

  • Friends love inside jokes
  • People are competitive
  • Users wanted to continue playing
With our findings and input from our Daimler liaisons, we settled on developing a unique in-vehicle trivia game.

Design

The main aspects of our design were that it fit nicely within Daimler's existing head unit UI, and that it felt like an exciting, futuristic game. As lead product designer, I took charge of designing the low-fi wireframes as well as high-fi working prototypes and components. This is our clickable Figma prototype.

Solution

Finally, we used React and Firebase to engineer a fully-functional, dynamic trivia game complete with user profiles, scores and leaderboard, and a variety of categories, including a location-based one! Give our game a try here!
